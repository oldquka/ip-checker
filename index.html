What is my IP and why does it matter?

IP address identifies your device online. It lets websites, apps, and services know where to send and receive information.

ISP (Internet Service Provider) is the company that gives you internet access. Your IP is usually assigned by your ISP.

ASN (Autonomous System Number) is a unique identifier for networks that exchange internet traffic. Each ISP or large network has its ASN.

IPv4 and IPv6 are two protocols for addressing devices. IPv4 is the older, most common format; IPv6 is newer and offers more addresses.

Your IP address and location are only approximate and cannot reveal your physical address.

FAQ

▾

Q1: What is “My IP”?
A1: Your IP address is a numerical label assigned by your ISP that identifies your device on the Internet.

Q2: How accurate is the location data?
A2: We use industry-standard geolocation APIs—accuracy is generally city-level, not a precise street address.

Q3: Do you store or share my IP?
A3: No. This is a purely client-side/static tool; we do not log, store, or transmit your IP beyond the API calls needed to display it.

Q4: Why show me ASN and ISP?
A4: ASN (Autonomous System Number) identifies the network your ISP operates. This can help troubleshoot routing or performance issues.

Q5: Can I integrate this on my own site?
A5: Yes—feel free to fork our code on GitHub and adapt it for your needs under the MIT license.
